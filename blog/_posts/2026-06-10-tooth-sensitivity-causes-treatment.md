## That Sharp, Shooting Pain

You sip cold water or bite into ice cream and a sharp electric jolt shoots through a tooth. Tooth sensitivity affects nearly half of adults — and most simply avoid cold foods instead of fixing the actual problem.

## Why Teeth Become Sensitive

Your teeth have a protective enamel coat. Beneath it lies **dentin**, filled with thousands of microscopic tubes leading directly to the nerve. When enamel wears away or gums recede, these tubes are exposed — and hot, cold, sweet, or sour triggers reach the nerve directly.

## Common Causes

**1. Aggressive brushing** — scrubbing hard with a hard-bristled brush literally wears grooves into teeth at the gum line. More pressure does NOT mean cleaner teeth.

**2. Gum recession** — gum disease or hard brushing exposes the root surface, which has no enamel protection at all.

**3. Acid erosion** — frequent colas, citrus juices, pickles, and acid reflux dissolve enamel.

**4. Teeth grinding (bruxism)** — night-time grinding wears enamel flat. Often unnoticed until a partner mentions the sound.

**5. Cracked tooth or leaking filling** — sensitivity localised to one tooth often signals a crack or decay.

**6. Recent dental work** — temporary sensitivity after fillings or cleaning is normal and fades within weeks.

## Treatments That Actually Work

**At home:**
- **Desensitising toothpaste** — blocks the exposed tubes. Use consistently for 2–4 weeks; spit, don't rinse, after brushing
- Switch to a **soft-bristled brush** and gentle circular strokes
- Avoid brushing immediately after acidic foods — wait 30 minutes

**At the clinic:**
- **Fluoride varnish** — strengthens enamel and seals tubes instantly
- **Bonding** — covering exposed root surfaces with tooth-coloured material
- **Night guard** — for grinders, protecting teeth while you sleep
- **Treating the underlying cause** — gum treatment, filling replacement, or crack repair

## When Sensitivity is a Red Flag

See us promptly if sensitivity:

- Lingers more than 30 seconds after the trigger
- Affects a single specific tooth
- Comes with pain on biting
- Wakes you at night

These signal deeper problems — possibly needing a filling or root canal — that get more expensive the longer you wait.
