## You Are What You Eat — So Are Your Teeth

Every bite either feeds the bacteria that harm your teeth or provides nutrients that strengthen them. Small dietary swaps can dramatically reduce cavities and keep your gums healthy.

## Best Foods for Your Teeth

**Cheese, milk, and curd** — rich in calcium and phosphates that strengthen enamel. Cheese also raises mouth pH, neutralising acid. A great snack.

**Crunchy vegetables and fruits** — apples, carrots, cucumber, and celery act like natural toothbrushes, scrubbing teeth and stimulating saliva.

**Leafy greens** — spinach and other greens deliver calcium and folic acid for healthy gums.

**Nuts** — almonds and peanuts provide calcium and protein with very little sugar.

**Eggs and fish** — vitamin D helps your body absorb calcium; fish like salmon support gum health.

**Water** — the best drink for teeth. It rinses away food, dilutes acid, and (when fluoridated) strengthens enamel.

**Green tea** — contains compounds that reduce harmful bacteria.

## Worst Foods for Your Teeth

**Sticky sweets** — chocolates with caramel, toffees, and Indian sweets cling to teeth, prolonging acid attacks.

**Colas and soft drinks** — a double attack of sugar plus acid. Even "diet" versions are highly acidic. Continuous sipping is the worst habit.

**Citrus and pickles** — frequent acidic foods erode enamel over time.

**Frequent snacking** — it's not just *what* but *how often*. Each snack restarts a 20–30 minute acid attack. Grazing all day keeps teeth under constant acid.

**Sticky dried fruits** — raisins and dates are healthy but cling to teeth; rinse after.

**Refined carbs** — biscuits, white bread, and chips break down into sugars that feed bacteria.

**Tea and coffee with sugar** — sugary hot drinks sipped slowly bathe teeth in sugar for long periods, plus they stain.

## Smart Eating Habits

- Eat sweets **with meals**, not as standalone snacks — more saliva is present to neutralise acid
- **Rinse with water** after every meal and snack
- Wait **30 minutes before brushing** after acidic foods
- Choose water over juice and cola
- End meals with cheese or a crunchy vegetable
- Don't put children to bed with milk or juice bottles

## The Bottom Line

You don't have to give up everything you love. Just be mindful of **frequency**, rinse with water, and keep up your brushing routine. Your teeth will thank you for decades.
