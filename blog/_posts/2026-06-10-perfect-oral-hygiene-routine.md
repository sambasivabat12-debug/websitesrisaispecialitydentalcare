## Most People Brush Wrong

You've brushed your teeth your whole life — but technique matters more than effort. After 35+ years of practice, we see the same preventable damage daily: worn enamel from hard scrubbing, cavities between teeth from never flossing, and gum disease from missed gum lines.

Here is the complete routine we teach our own families.

## Morning Routine

**1. Brush for 2 full minutes** — most people stop at 45 seconds. Divide your mouth into 4 quarters, 30 seconds each.

**2. The correct technique:** hold the brush at **45 degrees to the gum line** and use small, gentle circular motions. Never saw back and forth horizontally.

**3. Don't forget:** inner surfaces of front teeth (the most-missed spot), chewing surfaces, and along the gum line.

**4. Clean your tongue** — scrape from back to front 3–4 times. This single habit transforms your breath.

**5. Spit, don't rinse** — leaving a thin fluoride film strengthens enamel all morning.

## Night Routine (The Most Important One)

While you sleep, saliva flow drops dramatically — bacteria multiply unchecked for 7–8 hours. Going to bed without brushing is the fastest route to cavities.

**1. Floss first** — brushing cleans only 65% of tooth surfaces. Curve the floss in a C-shape around each tooth and slide gently below the gum line.

**2. Brush for 2 minutes** with fluoride toothpaste.

**3. Nothing after brushing** except water. That bedtime biscuit or glass of milk feeds bacteria all night.

## Choosing the Right Tools

- **Brush:** soft bristles, small head. Hard brushes damage enamel and gums
- **Replace every 3 months** or when bristles splay
- **Toothpaste:** any fluoride toothpaste; whitening pastes are fine but won't change tooth colour significantly
- **Electric brushes** help people who brush too hard or rush — but a manual brush used correctly is equally effective
- **Mouthwash:** optional, not a substitute for brushing or flossing

## Habits That Quietly Damage Teeth

- Opening packets and bottle caps with teeth
- Chewing ice and pen caps
- Sipping sugary tea/coffee continuously through the day
- Using toothpicks aggressively (use floss instead)
- Brushing immediately after acidic food — wait 30 minutes

## The 6-Month Rule

Even perfect brushing can't remove hardened tartar — only professional cleaning can. Visit every 6 months so small problems are caught while they're still small and inexpensive.
