## Modern Dentures Are Not What You Remember

Forget the loose, uncomfortable false teeth of decades past. Today's dentures are precisely fitted, natural-looking, and comfortable — restoring your ability to eat, speak, and smile confidently.

## Types of Dentures

**Complete dentures** — replace all teeth in the upper or lower jaw. Custom-made to fit your gums precisely.

**Partial dentures** — replace several missing teeth while clasping onto your remaining natural teeth. They prevent remaining teeth from shifting.

**Flexible dentures** — made from soft, flexible material. No metal clasps, more comfortable, virtually invisible in the mouth.

**Implant-supported dentures** — the premium option. The denture clips onto 2–4 implants, eliminating slipping completely. You can eat anything with full confidence.

## Why Replace Missing Teeth with Dentures?

- **Eat properly again** — poor chewing affects digestion and nutrition
- **Speak clearly** — missing teeth cause slurred or whistling speech
- **Support facial muscles** — prevents the sunken, aged look
- **Smile confidently** in family photos and gatherings

## The Denture Process

1. **Examination** of your gums and any remaining teeth
2. **Impressions** taken of your jaws
3. **Trial fitting** — we check the look and bite with a wax try-in
4. **Final denture delivery** — adjusted for perfect comfort
5. **Follow-up visits** for fine adjustments as you adapt

The full process takes about 1–2 weeks.

## Adjusting to New Dentures

The first 2–4 weeks involve adaptation. Start with soft foods cut into small pieces, chew on both sides, and practice reading aloud to adjust speech. Mild soreness early on is normal — we adjust the fit at follow-up visits.

## Denture Care Tips

- Remove and rinse after eating
- Brush dentures daily with a soft brush (not regular toothpaste — it scratches)
- Soak in water or denture solution overnight — never let them dry out
- Clean your gums and tongue daily
- Visit us yearly to check the fit, as gums change shape over time
