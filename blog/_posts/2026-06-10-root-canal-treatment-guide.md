## What is Root Canal Treatment?

Root canal treatment (RCT) saves a severely infected or damaged tooth instead of removing it. When the pulp — the soft tissue inside your tooth containing nerves and blood vessels — becomes infected, a root canal removes it, cleans the inside of the tooth, and seals it permanently.

The result? Your natural tooth is saved, pain is eliminated, and you can chew normally again.

## Why Do You Need a Root Canal?

You may need root canal treatment if you have:

- **Severe toothache** that doesn't go away, especially when chewing
- **Prolonged sensitivity** to hot or cold food and drinks
- **Darkening or discolouration** of the tooth
- **Swelling or tenderness** in nearby gums
- **A pimple-like bump** on the gums near the painful tooth
- A **cracked or chipped tooth** that has become infected

## Is Root Canal Treatment Painful?

**No.** Root canal has an unfair reputation. The pain people associate with RCT is actually from the **infection** — not the treatment. ## The Procedure — Step by Step

1. **Examination and digital X-ray** to see the root canals and infection
2. **Local anaesthesia** to completely numb the area
3. **Removing the infected pulp** through a small opening
4. **Cleaning and disinfecting** the canals thoroughly
5. **Filling the canals** with biocompatible gutta-percha
6. **Crown placement** to protect the tooth and restore full function

The entire procedure takes 1 to 2 appointments depending on infection severity.

## Root Canal vs Tooth Extraction

Saving your natural tooth is always better. When a tooth is extracted, surrounding teeth shift, bone loss begins, and you need a costly implant or bridge to replace it. Root canal preserves your natural tooth and is more cost-effective long term.
