## Dental Emergencies Can't Wait

Knowing what to do in the first few minutes of a dental emergency can save a tooth — or save you from severe complications. Save our number now: **86880 33072.**

## Knocked-Out Tooth (Avulsion)

This is a true emergency — **you have 30–60 minutes** to save the tooth.

1. **Pick up the tooth by the crown** (top), never the root
2. If dirty, **rinse gently** with milk or water — do NOT scrub or remove tissue
3. **Try to reinsert it** into the socket and bite on a clean cloth
4. If you can't reinsert, **store it in milk** (or inside the cheek, or saline) — never in plain water or dry
5. **Reach us immediately**

A tooth placed back within the hour has the best chance of survival.

## Broken or Chipped Tooth

1. Save any broken pieces in milk
2. Rinse your mouth with warm water
3. Apply a cold compress externally to reduce swelling
4. Cover any sharp edge with dental wax or sugar-free gum
5. See us promptly — even painless chips can expose the nerve

## Severe Toothache

1. Rinse with warm salt water
2. Floss gently to remove any trapped food
3. Take a normal painkiller (paracetamol/ibuprofen) — **never place aspirin directly on the gum**, it burns the tissue
4. Cold compress on the cheek for swelling
5. **Do not apply heat** — it worsens infection

Severe pain usually means the nerve is involved (root canal territory). Don't wait it out.

## Facial Swelling — Take This Seriously

Swelling of the face, gum, or under the jaw signals a spreading infection. Swelling near the eye or that makes swallowing/breathing difficult is a **medical emergency** — go to a hospital immediately. Dental infections can become life-threatening if ignored.

## Broken Braces Wire

If a wire is poking your cheek, cover the end with orthodontic wax or a cotton ball. Don't cut it. Book a visit to fix it properly.

## Lost Filling or Crown

Keep the crown safe. Avoid chewing on that side. Temporary dental cement from a pharmacy can protect the tooth until you reach us. Don't use superglue.

## Prevent Emergencies

- Wear a mouthguard for contact sports
- Don't chew ice, hard nuts, or popcorn kernels
- Never use teeth as tools
- Treat small cavities before they become painful emergencies
