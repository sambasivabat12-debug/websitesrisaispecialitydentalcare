## What Are Wisdom Teeth?

Wisdom teeth (third molars) are the last teeth to erupt, usually between ages 17 and 25. Most jaws don't have enough space for them — which is when problems begin.

## Signs Your Wisdom Tooth Needs Attention

- Pain or swelling at the back of your jaw
- Red, swollen, or bleeding gums around the back teeth
- Difficulty opening your mouth fully
- Bad breath or bad taste from food trapped around the tooth
- Headache or ear pain on one side
- Swollen lymph nodes under the jaw

## What is an Impacted Wisdom Tooth?

When a wisdom tooth doesn't have room to erupt normally, it gets **impacted** — stuck under the gum or growing at an angle, pushing against the neighbouring tooth. Impacted teeth can cause:

- Repeated infections (pericoronitis)
- Damage and decay to the healthy tooth next to it
- Cysts that damage the jawbone
- Crowding of front teeth

## Does Every Wisdom Tooth Need Removal?

No. If a wisdom tooth has erupted fully, is positioned correctly, and you can clean it properly — it can stay. Removal is recommended when there is repeated pain, infection, decay, impaction, or damage to nearby teeth. An X-ray tells us exactly what's happening below the gum.

## Recovery Tips After Removal

- Bite on the gauze for 45–60 minutes to stop bleeding
- Apply ice packs externally for the first 24 hours
- Eat soft, cool foods — curd rice, ice cream, smoothies
- **Do not** spit forcefully, use straws, or smoke for 3 days
- Take prescribed medication on time
- Most people return to work the next day
