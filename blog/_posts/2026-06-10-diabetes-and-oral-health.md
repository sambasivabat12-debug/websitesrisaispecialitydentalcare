## A Dangerous Two-Way Street

Diabetes and oral health are deeply connected — and the relationship runs both ways. High blood sugar worsens gum disease, and gum infection makes blood sugar harder to control. Understanding this cycle can protect both your teeth and your overall health.

## How Diabetes Affects Your Mouth

**Higher gum disease risk** — diabetics are 2–3 times more likely to develop gum disease. High glucose in saliva feeds bacteria, and impaired immunity reduces your ability to fight infection.

**Slower healing** — extractions, surgery, and gum treatment heal more slowly when sugar is uncontrolled.

**Dry mouth** — reduced saliva increases cavities and discomfort.

**Fungal infections** — oral thrush is more common in diabetics.

**More cavities** — dry mouth plus high sugar accelerates decay.

## How Gum Disease Affects Diabetes

This is the part most people don't know: **untreated gum infection raises blood sugar.** The chronic inflammation makes your body more resistant to insulin, pushing your HbA1c higher. Studies show that treating gum disease can **improve blood sugar control** — sometimes as effectively as adding a medication.

So treating your gums isn't just about your smile — it helps manage your diabetes.

## Warning Signs Diabetics Should Watch For

- Bleeding, red, or swollen gums
- Gums pulling away from teeth
- Persistent bad breath
- Loose teeth
- Dry mouth or burning sensation
- White patches (possible thrush)

## Dental Care Tips for Diabetics

- **Control your blood sugar** — the single most important step for oral health
- **Tell us your HbA1c** and medications at every visit
- **Get cleaning every 3–6 months** — more frequent than non-diabetics
- **Brush twice and floss daily** without fail
- **Schedule morning appointments** when blood sugar is most stable
- **Eat before dental visits** and bring your medication
- **Stay hydrated** to combat dry mouth
- **Quit tobacco** — it multiplies every diabetic oral risk

## Can Diabetics Get Implants and Major Treatment?

Yes — controlled diabetics can safely have extractions, implants, and surgery. The key is **well-managed blood sugar** for good healing. We coordinate care carefully for diabetic patients, and with 35+ years of experience, we handle these cases routinely and safely.
