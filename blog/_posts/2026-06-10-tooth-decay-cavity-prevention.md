## How Does a Cavity Form?

Your mouth hosts bacteria that feed on sugars from food. As they feed, they produce **acid** that dissolves tooth enamel. Each sugary snack triggers an acid attack lasting 20–30 minutes. Over time, the acid creates a hole — a cavity.

**Sugar frequency matters more than quantity.** Sipping cola all afternoon is far worse than drinking it once with a meal.

## The Stages of Tooth Decay

**Stage 1 — White spot:** the earliest sign; enamel is losing minerals. Reversible with fluoride.

**Stage 2 — Enamel cavity:** a hole forms. No pain yet — this is the cheapest, easiest stage to fill.

**Stage 3 — Dentin decay:** decay reaches the softer layer beneath enamel. Sensitivity to sweets and cold begins. Still treatable with a filling.

**Stage 4 — Pulp infection:** decay reaches the nerve. Severe pain, especially at night. Now requires **root canal treatment**.

**Stage 5 — Abscess:** infection spreads beyond the root, causing swelling and serious risk. May require extraction.

The lesson: **a ₹1,500 filling today prevents a ₹8,000 root canal tomorrow.**

## Why Don't Cavities Hurt at First?

Enamel has no nerves. By the time a tooth hurts, decay has already reached deep inside. This is why 6-monthly check-ups matter — we catch cavities at stage 1–2 when treatment is simple and inexpensive.

## Modern Tooth-Coloured Fillings

Today's composite fillings match your tooth colour exactly and bond directly to the tooth. The procedure is quick:

1. Numb the area (small cavities often need no anaesthesia)
2. Remove decay
3. Fill with composite, shaped and hardened with light
4. Polish — done in 20–30 minutes

## Proven Prevention Strategies

- **Brush twice daily** with fluoride toothpaste — especially at night
- **Floss daily** — cavities between teeth are invisible until large
- **Limit sugar frequency** — keep sweets to mealtimes
- **Rinse with water** after every meal or snack
- **Chew sugar-free gum** to stimulate protective saliva
- **Dental sealants** for children's molars
- **Check-ups every 6 months** to catch decay early
