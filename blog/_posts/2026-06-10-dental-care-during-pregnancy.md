## Why Pregnancy Affects Your Teeth

Pregnancy hormones change your mouth significantly. Many women are surprised by sudden gum bleeding, sensitivity, or swelling. Caring for your teeth during pregnancy protects not just you, but your baby too — research links gum disease to premature birth and low birth weight.

## Pregnancy Gingivitis

Up to 70% of pregnant women develop **pregnancy gingivitis** — red, swollen, bleeding gums caused by hormonal surges making gums more reactive to plaque.

**Manage it by:**
- Brushing twice daily, gently along the gum line
- Flossing daily
- Getting a professional cleaning — safe during pregnancy

Don't stop brushing because gums bleed — that makes it worse.

## Morning Sickness and Your Teeth

Stomach acid from vomiting erodes enamel. Protect your teeth:

- **Don't brush immediately** after vomiting — acid-softened enamel scrubs away. Wait 30 minutes
- Rinse with water mixed with a pinch of baking soda to neutralise acid
- Then brush gently later

## Is Dental Treatment Safe During Pregnancy?

**Yes — necessary treatment is safe and important.** Ignoring an infection is far riskier to the baby than treating it.

**The second trimester (4–6 months) is the ideal time** for any needed dental work — you're past early-pregnancy nausea and comfortable enough to sit through treatment.

**Safe during pregnancy:**
- Professional cleaning and scaling
- Fillings for cavities
- Root canal if needed (better than letting infection spread)
- Tooth extraction if necessary
- Dental X-rays with abdominal shielding (radiation is minimal and directed away from the abdomen)

**Best postponed to after delivery:**
- Elective cosmetic work (whitening, veneers)
- Non-urgent procedures

## Always Tell Your Dentist

Inform us that you're pregnant and how far along, plus any medications or complications. We'll tailor treatment and choose pregnancy-safe medicines.

## Sugar Cravings and Cavities

Frequent snacking and sweet cravings raise cavity risk. Choose teeth-friendly snacks (cheese, nuts, fruit), rinse after eating, and keep up your brushing routine even when exhausted.

## Caring for Two

A mother's oral bacteria can transfer to her baby after birth. Healthy teeth now means a healthier start for your child. A dental check-up is a wonderful item to tick off your pregnancy to-do list.
