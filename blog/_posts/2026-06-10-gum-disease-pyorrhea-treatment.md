## Bleeding Gums Are Never Normal

If your gums bleed when you brush, that's your body's alarm bell. Gum disease is the **leading cause of tooth loss in adults** — more than cavities. The tragedy? It's almost entirely preventable when caught early.

## The Two Stages of Gum Disease

**Stage 1 — Gingivitis (reversible)**

Plaque builds up at the gum line, causing inflammation:

- Red, swollen gums
- Bleeding while brushing or eating
- Bad breath

At this stage, professional cleaning and good home care can **completely reverse** the condition.

**Stage 2 — Periodontitis / Pyorrhea (irreversible damage)**

Untreated gingivitis advances below the gum line. The infection destroys the bone holding your teeth:

- Gums pull away from teeth, forming deep pockets
- Persistent bad breath and bad taste
- Pus between teeth and gums
- Teeth become loose and drift apart
- Eventually, teeth fall out — even healthy-looking ones

Bone lost to pyorrhea **does not grow back**. Treatment can only stop further destruction.

## What Causes Gum Disease?

- Plaque and tartar from inadequate brushing and flossing
- **Smoking and tobacco** — the single biggest risk multiplier
- **Diabetes** — uncontrolled sugar dramatically worsens gum disease
- Hormonal changes (pregnancy, menopause)
- Family history

## Treatment Options

**Scaling and polishing** — professional removal of plaque and tartar above and below the gum line. The foundation of all gum treatment.

**Deep cleaning (root planing)** — for deeper pockets, cleaning the root surfaces so gums can reattach.

**Gum surgery** — for advanced cases, to reduce pockets and clean inaccessible areas.

## Does Scaling Loosen Teeth? (Myth Busted)

No! This is the most common myth in India. Scaling removes the hard tartar that was *covering up* looseness caused by bone loss. The cleaning reveals the damage — it doesn't cause it. Delaying scaling because of this myth allows pyorrhea to silently destroy more bone.

## Protect Your Gums Daily

- Brush twice daily along the gum line at 45 degrees
- Floss or use interdental brushes daily — brushing alone misses 35% of tooth surfaces
- Quit tobacco in all forms
- Control blood sugar if diabetic
- Get professional cleaning every 6 months
