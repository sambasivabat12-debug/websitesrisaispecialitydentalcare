## "But My Teeth Don't Hurt"

This is the most common reason people skip the dentist — and the most costly mistake in dentistry. Here's the hard truth: **by the time a tooth hurts, the problem is already advanced.** Pain is a late warning sign, not an early one.

Regular 6-monthly check-ups catch problems while they're small, painless, and cheap to fix.

## Why Waiting for Pain Costs You More

Consider the same cavity over time:

- **Caught early (check-up):** a quick, simple filling — about 20 minutes
- **Ignored 6 months:** decay reaches the nerve — now needing a root canal plus a crown
- **Ignored further:** tooth lost — now needing a far more costly implant or bridge

A check-up costs a fraction of what one ignored problem costs to repair. Prevention is always cheaper than cure.

## What Happens at a Check-up?

**1. Cavity detection** — we find decay at the early, paint-free stage, often between teeth where you can't see.

**2. Gum assessment** — checking for early gum disease (the leading cause of tooth loss) before bone is lost.

**3. Professional cleaning** — removing hardened tartar that brushing simply cannot remove, preventing gum disease and bad breath.

**4. Oral cancer screening** — we check your tongue, cheeks, and tissues. Early detection of oral cancer — common in tobacco-using populations — saves lives.

**5. Bite and wear check** — spotting grinding, cracks, and failing old fillings before they break.

**6. X-rays when needed** — revealing hidden decay, infections, and bone levels invisible to the naked eye.

## The Hidden Health Connection

Your mouth is a window to your overall health. Dental check-ups can reveal early signs of diabetes, vitamin deficiencies, and other conditions. Gum disease is linked to heart disease, stroke, diabetes complications, and pregnancy issues. Caring for your mouth protects your whole body.

## Who Needs More Frequent Visits?

Some people benefit from check-ups every 3–4 months:

- Diabetics
- Smokers and tobacco users
- People with a history of gum disease
- Pregnant women
- Those with braces

## Make It a Habit

Tie your dental check-up to something memorable — your birthday and half-birthday, or a festival. Two visits a year is all it takes to keep your natural teeth healthy for life.
