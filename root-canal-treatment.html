Treatment Overview

What is Root Canal Treatment?

Root canal treatment (RCT), also called endodontic therapy, is a procedure to save a tooth that has severely infected or inflamed pulp — the soft tissue inside the tooth. During treatment, the infected pulp is removed, the canal is cleaned and shaped with precision rotary instruments, and then sealed to prevent re-infection. A crown is usually placed afterward for full protection.

At Sri Sai Speciality Dental Care, Dr. T. Venkat Rao and Dr. T. Sai Vakula have performed thousands of successful root canal treatments. With modern single-visit techniques and effective anaesthesia, the procedure is far more comfortable than most patients expect.

The Procedure

How Root Canal Treatment Works at Our Clinic

1
Examination & X-Ray

Dr. T. Venkat Rao or Dr. T. Sai Vakula examines your tooth and takes a digital X-ray to assess the extent of infection and the canal anatomy before planning treatment.

2
Local Anaesthesia

A local anaesthetic is applied to completely numb the tooth and surrounding area. You will feel pressure but no pain during the procedure.

3
Pulp Removal

A small opening is made in the crown of the tooth. The infected pulp tissue is carefully removed using precision rotary endodontic instruments.

4
Canal Cleaning & Shaping

The root canals are cleaned, shaped and disinfected with irrigating solutions to eliminate all bacteria and prevent re-infection.

5
Canal Filling & Sealing

The cleaned canals are filled with a biocompatible material called gutta-percha and sealed with dental cement to prevent future bacterial entry.

6
Crown Placement

A dental crown (zirconia or PFM) is placed over the treated tooth at a follow-up appointment to protect it, restore full biting function, and complete the smile.

FAQs

Root Canal — Common Questions

Is root canal treatment painful?
No. With modern local anaesthesia and gentle rotary endodontic instruments, root canal treatment at Sri Sai Speciality Dental Care is virtually painless. Most patients report it feels no different from having a regular filling. You may feel mild soreness for a day or two after treatment, which is normal.
How many visits does root canal treatment take?
Most root canals at our clinic are completed in a single visit of 45–90 minutes. Severely infected teeth or teeth with complex canal anatomy may require two visits — one for cleaning and temporary filling, and a second for permanent sealing and crown placement.
How much does root canal treatment cost in Karmanghat / Hyderabad?
The cost depends on your specific condition and the treatment you need. Please consult our doctor for an accurate, personalised estimate — call us at 86880 33072. We offer honest, transparent care with no hidden charges.
What happens if I don't get root canal treatment?
An untreated infected tooth will worsen over time. The infection can form a painful abscess, spread to neighbouring teeth, jawbone, or even the bloodstream (a serious condition). Eventually the tooth may need to be extracted. Replacing an extracted tooth with an implant or bridge costs significantly more than a root canal.
Do I need a crown after root canal treatment?
A crown is strongly recommended after root canal treatment, especially for back teeth (molars and premolars) that bear heavy chewing forces. The root-canal-treated tooth becomes more brittle over time without a crown and is at risk of fracture. A crown protects the tooth and restores full function and aesthetics.
How long does a root canal treated tooth last?
With proper oral hygiene and a crown placed over the tooth, a root-canal-treated tooth can last a lifetime. Regular dental checkups every 6 months help ensure the tooth remains healthy and functional for decades.
