What We Offer

What is a Dental Implant?

A dental implant is a small titanium post surgically placed into the jawbone to replace a missing tooth root. Once it fuses with the bone — a process called osseointegration — a natural-looking crown is fixed on top.

The result is a tooth that looks, feels, and functions exactly like your natural tooth. Studies show dental implants have a 97–98% success rate over 10+ years, making them the most reliable long-term solution for missing teeth.

Unlike bridges or dentures, implants stand alone — preserving adjacent teeth and stimulating the jawbone to prevent bone loss.

Are You a Candidate?

Who is a Good Candidate?

Most healthy adults with one or more missing teeth are ideal candidates for dental implants. Key factors our doctors evaluate:

  • Healthy gums — free from active gum disease
  • Sufficient bone — enough jawbone to support the implant (bone grafts can help if needed)
  • Good general health — conditions like diabetes should be managed first
  • Non-smoker — or willing to stop smoking before treatment
  • Commitment to hygiene — regular brushing, flossing and dental visits

Age is less important than health. Many senior patients get implants successfully. Our doctors will assess your suitability at a consultation.

Risks & Complications

What Are the Possible Risks?

Dental implants have a very high success rate, but like any surgical procedure, there are rare risks to be aware of. Our experienced surgeons minimise all of them.

🦠

Infection (Peri-implantitis)

The most common issue. Prevented with good oral hygiene, regular check-ups, and antibiotics when needed.

❌

Implant Failure (2–5%)

Rarely the implant may not integrate. A new implant can usually be placed after healing.

🧠

Nerve / Bone Injury

Incorrect placement can affect nearby nerves. Our 3D CT imaging before surgery minimises this risk significantly.

🚬

Smoking Risk

Smoking greatly increases failure risk by slowing healing. Patients are strongly advised to quit before surgery.

Common Questions

Frequently Asked Questions

Q Is dental implant surgery painful?
No. The procedure is performed under local anaesthesia — you feel pressure but no sharp pain during surgery. Most patients are surprised at how comfortable it is. Any soreness for 1–2 days after is easily managed with mild pain relief.
Q How long do dental implants last?
With proper oral hygiene and regular dental visits, implants can last a lifetime. Clinical studies show 97–99% of implants survive 10 years or more. The ceramic crown may need replacement after 10–15 years, but the implant itself is permanent.
Q How is an implant different from a bridge or denture?
Implants are the only option that replaces the tooth root. Bridges require drilling healthy adjacent teeth. Dentures can slip and don't prevent bone loss. Implants are standalone, preserve bone, protect neighbouring teeth, and feel completely natural.
Q What is the cost of dental implants in Hyderabad?
Cost depends on the number of implants, type of crown, and bone condition. At Sri Sai Speciality Dental Care, we offer transparent, affordable pricing with no hidden charges. Call us for a personalised quote: 86880 33072.
Q How many visits does implant treatment take?
Typically 3–4 visits over 4–6 months: (1) consultation and planning, (2) implant placement surgery, (3) a review during healing, and (4) crown placement. Complex cases may need additional visits.
Q Am I too old for dental implants?
There is no upper age limit. Many senior patients receive implants successfully. What matters is your general health and bone density, not your age. Implants are not recommended for children whose jaws are still growing.
Q Can I smoke after getting dental implants?
Smoking greatly increases the risk of implant failure by slowing healing and increasing infection. Patients are strongly advised to quit smoking before surgery and throughout the healing period.
Q What if my implant fails?
Early failure (before crown placement) means removing the implant, allowing the site to heal, and placing a new one after a few months. Late failures due to infection can often be treated and a new implant placed.
Q What is All-on-4?
All-on-4 is a technique to replace a full arch of teeth using just 4 implants. It is a permanent, fixed solution for patients with no teeth or failing teeth — often completed faster than traditional full-arch implants.
Q Can I get an implant the same day as extraction?
In some cases, yes — called an immediate implant. If the extraction site has enough healthy bone, an implant can be placed the same day. In other cases, we wait for the site to heal first. Our doctors will advise the best approach.
