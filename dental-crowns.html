What Are Dental Crowns

A Protective Cap That Looks Completely Natural

A dental crown is a custom-made cap placed over a damaged, broken, severely decayed or root canal-treated tooth — restoring its original shape, size, strength and appearance while protecting it from further damage.

Crowns completely cover the visible portion of the tooth above the gum line. With modern zirconia and ceramic materials, your crown blends invisibly with your natural teeth — nobody will know it's there.

🦷
Who is it for? Patients with broken, fractured, worn or heavily decayed teeth — and anyone who's had a root canal treatment.
⏱
How long does it take? Most crown treatments need just 2 visits — preparation and final placement.

The Procedure

Your Crown Treatment, Step by Step

From examination to final fitting — here's exactly how we restore your tooth in 2 comfortable visits.

1

Consultation & Examination

Dr. T. Venkat Rao or Dr. T. Sai Vakula assesses your tooth and confirms a crown is the best treatment.

2

Digital X-Rays

X-rays evaluate the tooth structure and surrounding tissues for a safe, predictable result.

3

Tooth Preparation

The tooth is gently reshaped under local anaesthesia to create space for your crown.

4

Impressions or Digital Scan

Precise measurements ensure your crown fits perfectly with your bite.

5

Temporary Crown

A temporary crown protects your tooth while the permanent one is being crafted.

6

Custom Fabrication

Your crown is custom-made to match the exact colour and shape of your natural teeth.

7

Final Placement

The permanent crown is fitted, adjusted for comfort, and securely cemented.

8

We verify your comfort, fit and bite alignment — and you leave with a stronger, beautiful tooth.

FAQs

Common Questions Answered

How long do dental crowns last?
+
Most crowns last 10–15 years or longer with good oral hygiene and regular dental check-ups.
Is getting a crown painful?
+
No. The procedure is comfortable and performed under local anaesthesia when needed — most patients find it completely routine.
Can crowns look natural?
+
Yes. Ceramic and zirconia crowns are colour-matched and shaped to blend invisibly with your natural teeth.
How many visits do I need?
+
Most crown treatments require just 2 visits — one for preparation and impressions, one for final placement.
Can I eat normally with a crown?
+
Yes. Once your permanent crown is cemented, you can eat and chew comfortably — just like a natural tooth.
Do crowns need special care?
+
No special routine — just brush twice daily, floss regularly and attend routine check-ups like you would for any tooth.
Why do I need a crown after a root canal?
+
Root canal-treated teeth become brittle over time. A crown protects the tooth from fracture and keeps it strong and functional for years.
